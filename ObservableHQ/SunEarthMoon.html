It is a very long story

  • The Earth rounds the Sun every 365 days
  • and the Moon rounds the Earth every 27 days

The Full Moon

The Full Moon happens at the Moon is opposite with the Sun.

From the Earth, people can see the full moon reflecting the sun light.

The Half Moon

The Half Moon happens after the full moon, after about 7 days.

From the Earth, people can see the light half of the moon.

The New Moon

The New Moon happens after the half moon, after about 7 days.

From the Earth, people can see a very limited piece of the lighting moon.

The Other Half Moon

The Other Half Moon happens after the new moon, after about 7 days.

From the Earth, people can see the other half of the first half moon.

The Full Moon Again

The Full Moon happens again after 30 days, a month.

The cycle happens every month.

The length of a month is 30, not 27, days

The Moon rounds at 27 days a circle.

But a month lasts about 30 days.

Why?

Answer:

The Moon does not light itself.

It reflects the Sun's light.

So, after it rounds the Earth a circle for 27 days.

The Earth goes forward about 360 * 27 / 365 degrees from the view of the Sun.

It will costs another about 3~4 days for the Moon to catch up.

As a result, a month refers the Earth SEE the Moon completes a circle.

It says, a month is about 30 days.
